DistributionSE Brazil (from S Minas Gerais to Rio Grande do Sul and Misiones; Bahia [HR 32: 61], Espírito Santo, Paraná, Rio de Janeiro, Santa Catarina, São Paulo),
Argentina (Misiones)

Type locality: Brazil.  
Reproductionoviparous 
TypesLectotype: MNHN-RA 3791, designated by Bernardo et al. 2012
Holotype: ZSM 631/1920, male [Clelia clathrata pulcherrima] 
DiagnosisO. clathratus is a single species that exhibits considerable variation in morphology, including three distinct patterns of coloration (melanistic, melanistic with inconspicuous narrow light bands, and with conspicuous dark and light bands alternating along the body) that overlap geographically. The morphology of the ornamented hemipenes varies between “T-shape” and “Y-shape” forms, which occur without any meaningful or elevational structuring. The loreals are usually absent. 
CommentSynonymy after CEI 1993 and BERNARDO et al. 2012. 
EtymologyNamed after Latin from clāthrī +‎ -ātus meaning grated, latticed (with bars); a reference to the dorsal pattern of the species.
