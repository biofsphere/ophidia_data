Higher TaxaBoidae (Boinae), Henophidia, Alethinophidia, Serpentes, Squamata (snakes)
Subspecies 
Common NamesE: Yellow Anaconda
G: Paraguay-Anakonda, Gelbe Anakonda
Portuguese: Boiguaçu, Curudiu, Curundiu, Quetomeniope, Sucuri, Sucuri-Amarela, Sucurijú, Sucuri-do-Pantanal, Sucuriu 
SynonymEunectes notaeus COPE 1862: 70
Eunectes murinus PERACCA 1895 (nec LINNAEUS)
Eunectes wieningeri STEINDACHNER 1903: 15
Eunectes notaeus — KAHL et al. 1980: 207
Eunectes notaeus — CEI 1993
Eunectes notaeus — LEYNAUD & BUCHER 1999: 14
Eunectes notaeus — MCDIARMID, CAMPBELL & TOURÉ 1999: 201
Eunectes notaeus — ZARACHO et al. 2014
Eunectes notaeus — WALLACH et al. 2014: 291
Eunectes notaeus — REYNOLDS & HENDERSON 2018: 22
Eunectes notaeus — NOGUEIRA et al. 2019 
DistributionBolivia, Paraguay, Uruguay, W Brazil (Mato Grosso, Mato Grosso do Sul, Rio Grande do Sul, Paraná [HR 27: 216], São Paulo), NE Argentina (Chaco, Corrientes, Entre Rios, Formosa, Misiones, Santa Fé, Corrientes, Santiago del Estero).

Type locality: Paraguay River and its tributaries  
Reproductionovovivparous 
TypesHolotype: lost, was USNM 4707 (lost fide Reynolds & Henderson 2018) 
Diagnosis 
CommentHybrids between Eunectes notaeus and E. murinus have been described and E. deschauenseei is extremely closely related to E. notaeus (fide DIRKSEN & BÖHME 1998).
