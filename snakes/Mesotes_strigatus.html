Reproductionovovivparous 
TypesHolotype: BMNH 1946.1.2.70, a 584 mm female (Warwick). 
DiagnosisCharacter table: Franco et al. 2017, Trevine et al. 2021: 238 (Table 1)

Diagnosis (Mesotes). “Dorsal scales smooth in 19/19/15 rows; stout head and body (average head length larger than 20 mm); dark blotches on labials sutures; well-defined and continuous dark longitudinal ventral stripes, the external pair more conspicuous; black tongue sheath and palate.” (Trevine et al. 2022, Appendix 3)

Synapomorphies (Mesotes). “Longitudinal ventral stripes continuous and defined; black tongue sheath and a black blotch on the oral palate; spines on hemipenial base restricted to minor delimited areas; postocular portion of the Harder gland completely covered by adductor musculature or by the Duvernoy gland; dorso-lateral crests of the parietal bone continue in a unique sharp central crest after the supraoccipital suture; free portion of the supratemporal bone surpasses the occipital condyle (Appendix S4–S5, Trevine et al. 2022). 
CommentSynonymy after CEI 1993. FREITAS 2014 lists Thamnodynastes nattereri (Mikan 1828) as valid while WALLACH et al. 2014 listed it as synonym of T. pallidus.

Distribution: Boos 1984 reported one record from Trinidad which has not been confirmed. See map in Nogueira et al. 2019.

Terra typica: India (GÜNTHER 1858, in error)

Habitat: partly arboreal (Harrington et al. 2018).
