DistributionNE/C Argentina (Buenos Aires, La Pampa, Santa Fe, Entre Rios, Corrientes, Misiones, Chaco, Formosa, Córdoba, Santiago del Estero, Catamarca, Tucumán, San Luis),
Uruguay, Paraguay, Brazil (Rio Grande do Sul, Santa Catarina, Paraná, São Paulo, Minas Gerais, Goiás [HR 32: 58], Mato Grosso do Sul, Rio de Janeiro)

Type locality: Paraguay and South America.  
Reproductionovovivparous 
TypesSyntypes: MNHN-RA 7744 and MNHN-RA 7765. (not unlocated as reported by MCDIARMID et al. 1999) 
DiagnosisScalation: There are 8-13 intersupraoculars, strongly keeled; 8-10 supralabials, none fused with the prelacunal; 12-14 infralabials; 25-35 (usually 27-31 in males and 29-33 in females) midbody dorsal scale rows; 155-183 ventrals in males and 164-190 in females; and 38-53 subcaudals in males and 30-44 in females. 
CommentVenomous! 
EtymologyNamed from Latin “alternatus”, meaning alternating, probably in reference to the staggered blotches on the side of the body.
